🚩 Top Red Flags
10 / 10
Blank cheque submission demanded
Asking candidates to submit signed blank cheques is a serious legal and financial risk. A company can fill in any amount later. This practice is widely used in unethical bond enforcement and may be illegal under Indian labour law (Payment of Wages Act, Contract Labour Act).
10 / 10
Original educational documents held as collateral
Retaining original 10th & 12th mark sheets prevents you from applying elsewhere for 2.6 years. This is a coercive retention tactic — India's Ministry of Labour has explicitly stated this is illegal under the Bonded Labour System (Abolition) Act, 1976.
9 / 10
2.6-year mandatory service bond
A 2.6-year lock-in for a ₹10,000/month internship is disproportionate. Legitimate companies offering genuine training rarely enforce such long bonds — and if they do, they compensate fairly. Courts in India frequently void one-sided service bonds as unenforceable.
8 / 10
₹10,000/month stipend for 6 months — below minimum wage in many states
For a B.Tech / MCA graduate doing real development work, ₹10,000/month is exploitative — especially tied to a 2.6-year bond. Industry standard for Python interns is ₹15,000–₹35,000/month even at small companies.
7 / 10
Post-training CTC of ₹2.5–3 LPA is well below market
Entry-level Python developers in India earn ₹3.5–6 LPA at the low end. ₹2.5 LPA after 6 months of training and a 2.6-year bond is significantly below market — you'd be locked in at a disadvantage.
6 / 10
No mention of bond penalty amount
The agreement mentions a 2.6-year service term but doesn't state the financial penalty for leaving early. Combined with blank cheques, this gives the company unchecked power to set the penalty amount retroactively.
5 / 10
Incomplete job location — no city mentioned
"G-84, Sector 63" without a city name is a minor opacity signal. Sector 63 is likely Noida, UP — but not stating it clearly makes relocation terms vague.

💬 5 Smart Interview Questions

Ask these if you still choose to interview — their answers will reveal intent. Click to send to Claude for follow-up coaching.

1
"Can you explain why the company needs blank cheques, and who is authorised to fill them in? Is there an indemnity clause protecting me if the cheque is misused?"
2
"What happens to my original mark sheets if I need to apply for a postgraduate programme or another job during the 2.6-year period? Can I get certified copies instead?"
3
"What is the specific penalty amount if I leave before the 2.6-year service period ends, and has your company ever taken legal action to enforce this bond?"
4
"Is the ₹2.5–3 LPA CTC fixed or a starting range? What is the typical salary progression after the first year, and are there performance-based increments?"
5
"Would the company be willing to replace the document and cheque requirements with a signed bond agreement witnessed by a notary, which would be legally equivalent but protect both parties?"
