    Quick Tips

    Pay yourself first. Move savings out the moment your stipend lands, before spending decisions happen.

    Round-number budgeting. Cap flexible categories like "fun" to a fixed number so tracking stays simple.

    Avoid lifestyle creep. If a future job raises your income, keep your student-era budget as the baseline for a while.

    Prompts to Ask Claude

    • "Help me build a 50/30/20 budget with a $900 monthly stipend."
    • "Explain how compound interest would grow a $50/month savings habit over 4 years."
    • "What's the difference between a high-yield savings account and a regular one?"
    • "Review my expense categories and tell me what looks unusually high for a student."

    Resources

    • Consumer Financial Protection Bureau — studentaid resources
    • Your campus financial aid office — ask about emergency micro-grants
    • A no-fee student checking account with a linked high-yield savings account
